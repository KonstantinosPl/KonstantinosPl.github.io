### About me

Konstantinos Plataridis is a PhD Candidate in the School of Civil Engineering at AUTh. He holds a bachelor's degree in Geology (AUTh) and an MSc in Environmental Protection and Sustainable Development (AUTh). His doctoral research focuses on the interaction between groundwater and land use changes, utilizing remote sensing and Artificial Intelligence (AI) methods. He has gained approximately 2 years of research experience through his involvement in EU-funded research projects (Horizon, Erasmus+) and has successfully completed a Code for Earth challenge run by the European Centre for Medium-Range Weather Forecasts (ECMWF). He is the first author of 2 publications in reputable scientific journals and 2 publications in peer-reviewed conference proceedings. His research interests include water resources management, groundwater management, AI methods in hydrological applications, and remote sensing techniques for hydrological studies.
