<h4 class="research-title">PhD Research</h4>
<p class="research-desc">
Investigating groundwater dynamics and hydroclimatic interactions using satellite observations (GRACE), remote sensing, geospatial analysis, and artificial intelligence. The research focuses on groundwater drought characterization, drought propagation, aquifer memory, and the validation of GRACE-derived groundwater storage anomalies using in situ groundwater observations.

<h4 class="research-title">Code for Earth 2024: Challenge 20</h4>
<p class="research-desc">Development of an open-source Python package for machine learning–based gap filling of daily streamflow time series. <h4 class="research-title">Hydroclimatic Trends in Greece</h4>
<p class="research-desc">Investigation of long-term hydroclimatic variability and climate extremes across Greece using the ERA5-Land reanalysis. The project analyzes trends in precipitation, temperature, evapotranspiration, runoff, soil moisture, and extreme climate indices at multiple spatial scales. To support reproducible analyses, the open-source Python package HydroTrends was developed for hydroclimatic trend detection, visualization, and spatial aggregation.</p>
